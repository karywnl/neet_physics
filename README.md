# NEET Physics

A friendly, visual guide to NEET physics, starting from absolute zero. The kind of explanation you wish someone had handed you on day one.

Read it here: https://karywnl.github.io/neet_physics/

## Why this exists

Most physics resources pull you in one of two directions. The cheat sheets give you formulas to memorise but never why they are true, so you are stuck the moment a question is phrased differently. The textbook is correct and complete, but it can feel cold and overwhelming when you are just finding your feet.

This guide tries to sit in the friendly middle. Every idea is explained three ways: what it is, why it exists, and why the formula is true. There are diagrams, plain language, small "predict the answer" checks written like real NEET questions, and the links between ideas are spelled out instead of left for you to stumble onto later.

A few things I cared about while writing it:

- You can stop at any depth. Each page opens with a one-line idea, then shows you how to use it, then goes as deep as you want into the derivation.
- Every formula and number is checked against the NCERT Class 11 Physics textbook before it goes in.
- It reads like a web, not a list. Every chapter ends by pointing to the ones it builds on, so you can wander in any direction.

## What is inside

1. Foundations: units and measurement
2. Kinematics: motion in a straight line, motion in a plane
3. Dynamics: laws of motion, work, energy and power
4. Rotational motion: particles and rotational motion
5. Gravitation

New to physics? Start with Foundations and read straight down.

Made as a personal learning project, shared in case it helps you too.
